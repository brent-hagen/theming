the dangers of circumstantial evidence
======================================

:: Description
The social consequences of the human weakness for sometimes putting undue
weight on circumstantial evidence is featured. Circumstantial evidence is
evidence that relies on an inference to connect it to a conclusion of fact.
This is to say that the conclusion does not follow with 100% certainty;
correctly gauging the degree of justified certainty may require careful
weighting of probabilities beyond what many people are capable of.

:: Parents
law and order issue

:: References
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circumstantial_evidence
